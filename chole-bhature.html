CHOLE BHATURE

Chole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish, consisting of spicy chickpeas (chole) served with deep-fried bread (bhature). It's a flavorful and filling dish, often enjoyed as a hearty lunch or dinner. The combination of spicy, tangy chickpeas with crispy, soft bhature is simply irresistible!

Chole is cooked with a mixture of spices like cumin, coriander, garam masala, and amchur (dry mango powder), while the bhature is made from refined flour, yogurt, and baking soda, giving it a soft, fluffy texture. This dish is typically served with onions, cucumber, and a dollop of yogurt on the side for added flavor.

Chole Bhature is a popular street food in India, especially in Delhi, Amritsar, and Punjab, and is a comfort food for many. It's perfect for gatherings, parties, or special occasions.



HOW TO PREPARE IT

1. Soak dried chickpeas (chole) overnight and cook them in a pressure cooker with water until soft.
2. In a pan, heat oil and saut√© onions, garlic, ginger, and green chilies until fragrant.
3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Then add a blend of spices like cumin, coriander, garam masala, red chili powder, and turmeric powder.
4. Add cooked chickpeas to the spice mixture and cook for 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
5. For the bhature, make a dough using all-purpose flour, yogurt, baking soda, and a pinch of salt. Let it rest for an hour.
6. Roll out small portions of dough and deep fry them until golden and puffed.
7. Serve the chole with the freshly made bhature, and enjoy this delicious, hearty meal.
