BIRYANI

Biryani is a fragrant, flavorful rice dish made with long-grain basmati rice, vegetables or meat, and a variety of aromatic spices. It is cooked through a method called layering, where the rice and cooked ingredients are layered and then steamed together.

This iconic dish is often enjoyed with raita, salad, or a boiled egg on the side. Biryani is a favorite at festivals, weddings, and family gatherings and is widely loved for its richness and variety.

Whether you prefer a vegetarian or non-vegetarian version, biryani is a perfect meal to indulge in when you're craving something hearty and satisfying.



HOW TO PREPARE IT

1. Begin by washing and soaking basmati rice for about 30 minutes.
2. Prepare the biryani base with spices like cumin, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, and bay leaves, along with onions, tomatoes, and yogurt.
3. Cook your choice of vegetables or meat (chicken, mutton, or paneer) with spices, and set them aside.
4. Layer the partially cooked rice with the meat or vegetable mixture in a pot.
5. Add fried onions, mint leaves, and saffron milk for extra flavor and color.
6. Steam the biryani on low heat for 15-20 minutes.
7. Serve hot with raita, salad, or pickle for a complete meal.
