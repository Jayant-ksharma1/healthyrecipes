DOSA

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram). The batter is spread thin on a hot griddle and cooked to form a crispy, golden-brown crepe.

It is typically served with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, and sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew). Dosa is a favorite breakfast or dinner option in many Indian homes and is loved for its crispy texture and savory flavors.

There are various types of dosas, such as plain dosa, masala dosa (with a spiced potato filling), and rava dosa (made from semolina), but the classic plain dosa remains a crowd favorite.



HOW TO PREPARE IT

1. Soak rice and urad dal separately for 4-5 hours and then grind them into a smooth batter.
2. Mix the ground batter and let it ferment overnight or for 8-12 hours.
3. Heat a griddle (tava) and grease it lightly with oil or ghee.
4. Pour a ladle of the fermented batter onto the griddle and spread it thin in a circular motion.
5. Drizzle oil or ghee on the edges and cook until the dosa turns golden and crispy.
6. Serve with coconut chutney, tomato chutney, and sambar for the perfect meal.
